MATH FRONTIER SEMINAR

First-order methods for solving convex (strongly) concave minimax problems with functional constraints

Xinhao Ying RPI MATH
Time 4:00 pm, 04/14
Location Amos Eaton 216
Food Pizza and wings

Abstract

Convex-concave minimax problems arise in many applications, including robust learning and Stackelberg models. Most first-order methods address unconstrained or projection-friendly settings, while functional constraints remain far less explored. In this talk, we will discuss a class of convex-(strongly)-concave minimax problems with functional constraints. Our approach is to reformulate the problem so that a proximal augmented Lagrangian framework can be applied. In particular, by exploiting strong duality, we incorporate the inner functional constraints into the objective. Each resulting subproblem is then solved by an inexact accelerated proximal gradient scheme to handle inexact gradients arising from approximately solving an auxiliary maximization subproblem. The main algorithmic framework is presented, and the iteration complexity for obtaining an \(\epsilon\)-KKT point is established.

About the Speaker

Xinhao Ying is a third-year PhD student in the Department of Mathematics at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute
